"Detection On the Basis of Overall Collected Ship Data Using Support Vector Regression"

It involves analyzing vessel trajectories to identify anomalous behavior patterns. By leveraging SVR, the model can predict expected trajectories and detect deviations from these predictions, indicating potential anomalies. This method utilizes various ship parameters, such as speed, course, and position, to build a robust prediction model. The result is enhanced maritime safety through early detection of irregularities that might signify mechanical issues, navigational errors, or other critical events.

"Detection On the Basis of Overall Collected Ship Data Using Logistic Regression"

It focuses on classifying ship behavior into distinct categories, such as normal or anomalous. By applying Logistic Regression, the model evaluates various ship parameters to determine the probability of each ship's status falling into these categories. This approach is particularly effective in identifying patterns and anomalies in maritime data, such as unusual speed changes or course deviations. The result is improved detection of potential issues, enabling timely interventions and enhancing overall maritime safety.
